REV B · 2025–PRESENT · ELECTRICAL / FIRMWARE

ClearNote

INDEPENDENT · OA-TECH SPONSOR SLOT — OPEN

A digital notepad built around E-Ink, for studying without a glowing rectangle begging for attention. One device, one job: paper that remembers. It’s also my research vehicle — ClearNote investigates how firmware architecture and hardware design choices affect responsiveness and power efficiency in a low-power E-Ink system.

E-Ink is slow and the ESP32-S3’s shared buses bottleneck easily. The whole project is about refusing to accept that: a multi-layer rigid-flex architecture and a deterministic FreeRTOS firmware strategy, so latency is something I design, not something I get.

2025–PRESENT · PRODUCTION ENGINEERING

The factory floor

WITH · CIRCUITHUB

At CircuitHub I do production engineering for the machines that build electronics at scale — Mycronic MY700 jet printers and the pick-and-place line around them. School teaches you to design a board; the floor teaches you everything that happens to it afterward, at speed, ten thousand times.

My own corner of it: I designed a build-down kit for the jet printers, and I’m inventing a labeling system so the line’s consumables — nozzles, gantries, pallets, panels, part-pickers — stop being anonymous. You can’t track wear on a thing that doesn’t have a name.

  • Build-down kit for the MY700 jet printers
  • Labeling system — nozzles → gantries → pallets & panels → PPRs
  • Selective solder programming on the ERSA
  • Production optimization research — unpublished, pending the company’s blessing

The floor is machines talking to each other all day. Back at school, I write the translations.

2026 · SOFTWARE / PROTOCOLS

Gig-E ↔ CAN FD

WITH · UMASS ROBOTICS

Robots have a language problem: the compute side speaks Gigabit Ethernet, the actuator side speaks CAN FD, and somebody has to translate without adding latency the control loop will feel. For our club’s Jetson-based platform, that somebody is me — parsing code that bridges the two buses, due before the fall build season.

  • Gigabit Ethernet ↔ CAN FD bridge / parsing layer
  • Target platform: NVIDIA Jetson + CAN-FD actuators
  • Hard deadline: SEP 2026 — integration before build season

Boards, factories, protocols — all of it is practice for the project I actually daydream about. It has feathers.

PHASE 1 OF 4 · 2026–2029 · ROBOTICS / BIOMIMICRY

Project Parrot

OA-TECH · THE LONG GAME

A robotic bird, built in phases across my entire degree. Not a drone with a costume — a machine that walks, then jumps, then flies the way a bird does, because biomimicry is the hardest version of every engineering problem at once: actuation, balance, weight, power, control.

Phase one is legs. Walking is already an honest fight — then jumping with enough force to self-propel, then ornithopter flight modeled in Isaac Sim before any feather touches hardware, and finally a vision and navigation brain. The target isn’t completion by graduation; it’s meaningful, documented progress. This page is the documentation.

  • Phase 1 — legs & walking (NOW)
  • Phase 2 — jumping: self-propulsion + balance in motion
  • Phase 3 — ornithopter flight, modeled first in ISAAC SIM
  • Phase 4 — onboard / offboard vision & navigation

Every project here stands on smaller, stranger ones. The archive keeps those too.
